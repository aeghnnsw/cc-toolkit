# Research Questions for Blood-Brain Barrier Permeability (BBBP) Prediction

## 10 Key Questions to Answer for Accurate BBBP Prediction

### 1. What physicochemical properties are most strongly correlated with BBBP?
- Molecular weight, lipophilicity (LogP/LogD), polar surface area (PSA), hydrogen bonding capacity, molecular flexibility, pKa, charge state

### 2. What are the different mechanisms by which molecules cross the blood-brain barrier?
- Passive transcellular diffusion, paracellular transport, carrier-mediated transport, receptor-mediated transcytosis, adsorptive-mediated transcytosis, active efflux

### 3. What proteins and transporters are involved in BBB penetration and efflux?
- P-glycoprotein (P-gp/MDR1), BCRP, MRP transporters, LAT1, GLUT1, transferrin receptor, LRP1, other ABC and SLC transporters

### 4. What molecular structural features are required or favorable for crossing the BBB?
- Optimal size range, lipophilic character, minimal hydrogen bond donors, specific functional groups, aromatic vs aliphatic structures, molecular shape and 3D conformation

### 5. How do experimental methods for measuring BBBP differ and what are their limitations?
- In vitro models (cell monolayers, artificial membranes), in vivo methods (brain/plasma ratios, microdialysis), in silico predictions, LogBB values, permeability coefficients

### 6. What role does plasma protein binding and brain tissue binding play in BBB permeability?
- Free drug hypothesis, unbound fraction in plasma vs brain, tissue partitioning, non-specific binding effects on effective permeability

### 7. How do species differences affect BBB permeability and prediction model generalizability?
- Interspecies transporter expression variations, physiological differences in BBB structure, relevance of animal models to human BBB

### 8. What existing computational models and descriptors have been most successful for BBBP prediction?
- Quantitative structure-property relationships (QSPR), machine learning approaches, molecular descriptors (topological, electronic, geometric), fingerprint representations

### 9. How does BBB permeability change under pathological conditions?
- BBB disruption in disease states (neuroinflammation, stroke, tumors, neurodegenerative diseases), impact on drug delivery, dynamic vs static BBB models

### 10. What are the key drug design rules and filters for optimizing CNS penetration?
- CNS MPO (Multi-Parameter Optimization), Lipinski Rule of 5 adaptations, golden triangle concept, relationship between BBB permeability and CNS efficacy vs side effects

---

**Purpose**: These questions guide comprehensive literature review and data analysis to build accurate BBBP prediction models with mechanistic understanding.
